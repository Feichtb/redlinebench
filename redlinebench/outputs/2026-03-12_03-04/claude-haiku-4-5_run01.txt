## STEP 3: SUMMARY

This 34-sheet architectural drawing set with an 11-page specification for a contemporary residential project contains **42 identifiable issues** distributed across all seven review categories. The most critical concerns are:

**1. Schedule-to-Specification Conflicts (8 issues: A-1, A-2, A-3, A-4, A-7, A-8, C-3, C-5):** The Room Finish Schedule does not align with specification material descriptions for multiple spaces (Entry Hall, Powder Room, Reading Nook, bathrooms), and floor/wall assemblies contradict finishes required by the written specifications. These conflicts will require RFIs or field decisions before construction can proceed.

**2. Code Compliance and Life Safety Gaps (10 issues: E-1 through E-10):** Critical code verification information is missing from the drawings, including stair riser/tread dimensions, handrail/guardrail details, emergency egress openings for bedrooms, hallway widths, bathroom accessibility clearances, attic access, and garage fire separation. Without these details, a building official will likely require additional documentation before permit issuance.

**3. Constructability and Design Incompleteness (11 issues: F-1 through F-11):** Multiple custom features (entry hall woodwork, reading nook bunk bed, master closet built-ins, kitchen shelving) lack specific construction details and dimensions. Critical building systems (radiant heating, hydronic manifold location, network equipment installation, pool integration) are referenced but not detailed. Appliance dimensions and clearances are not verified. Garage HVAC design is deferred as "TBD." These gaps will generate significant field coordination issues and change orders.

These issues are symptomatic of a **bid-stage document that should not yet be released to contractors** without substantial completion of design details, specification-drawing reconciliation, and code compliance verification.
