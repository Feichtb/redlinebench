- **Project:** Bonfire House, Chatham County, NC; 2018 NC Residential Code (IRC 2015 with amendments); Chatham County Building Inspections
- **Building type:** Single-family residence; Occupancy R-3; Construction Type V-B
- **Climate zone:** 4A (stated in G0.11)
- **Drawing set scope:** Architectural sheets only — G, A1, A2, A3, A4, A5, A6, A7, A8 series. Structural, MEP, civil, and landscape drawings not included.
- **Specifications:** 11-page project specifications document provided covering all divisions relevant to this project.

### B — Dimensional and Geometric Integrity

B-1 | A3.22 Sheet 2, Section Stairs South | The south stair section shows Level 1 datum at 508'-0" and Level 2 datum at 518'-0" (10'-0" floor-to-floor) with "8 EQ RISERS," which would require a riser height of 15" — geometrically impossible and more than double the maximum IRC riser height of 7-3/4". | Certain

B-2 | A3.22 Sheet 1, Section Stairs East | The east stair section shows "10 EQ RISERS / 9 EQ TREADS" between the Level 1 and Level 2 datums (10'-0" floor-to-floor); with a labeled riser height of 6-11/16", 10 risers yield a total rise of approximately 5'-7", which does not match the 10'-0" floor-to-floor distance — the section datum labels and riser count are irreconcilable as drawn. | Certain

B-3 | A3.01, A3.02, A3.03, A3.10 | The crawl space floor datum elevation is inconsistently labeled across exterior elevation and section sheets: A3.10 Section North shows "502'-0"," A3.02 North Elevation shows "501'-0"," A3.03 West Elevation Partial shows "502'-0"" — the crawl space datum varies by 1'-0" between sheets. | Certain

### C — Specification-to-Drawing Alignment

C-1 | A8.01 Window Schedule; Specifications page 3 | The window schedule specifies Marvin Ultrex product codes and Ultrex-Marvin-Stonewhite framing for all window types, while the project specifications explicitly require Andersen 400 Series Casement and Fixed windows — a direct and irreconcilable product conflict between the two primary contract documents. | Certain

C-2 | Specifications pages 2–3 | The project specifications assign Exterior Wood Cladding WD-3 (Thermory Ash) to "South and West Facades" and simultaneously assign Hardieplank Lap Siding to "North and West Facades," with both products assigned to the west facade and no delineation of which areas of that facade receive each cladding material. | Certain

C-3 | A4.11 Sheet 4, A4.12 Sheet 6; Specifications page 3 | Exterior detail drawings consistently call out "VENTED FIBERBOARD SOFFIT" at all overhang conditions, while the project specifications require "Hardiesoffit panels, Smooth, Primed" — fiber cement (Hardiesoffit) and wood-based fiberboard are different materials with different moisture performance characteristics. | Certain

C-4 | A4.00 E-1 Wall Assembly; G0.11 Code Summary; Specifications page 2 | The E-1 CMU wall assembly on A4.00 shows "1/2" RIGID INSULATION @ CRAWL SPACE" on the interior face of the foundation wall, providing approximately R2.5; the code summary requires crawl space wall R=10 (interior) and the project specifications state "Stem Wall: 2" rigid XPS = R10" — the drawn assembly provides less than one-quarter of the required insulation value. | Certain

C-5 | A4.00 E-4 Wall Assembly; Specifications page 4 | The project specifications describe the wall insulation system as "R21 5.5" fiberglass batts + R6 1" Insulated ZIP Board," where Insulated ZIP Board is a structural sheathing product combining OSB and rigid foam; wall assembly E-4 on A4.00 shows conventional 1/2" plywood sheathing plus a separate 1-1/2" rigid insulation board — these are not equivalent assemblies and the drawing does not use the specified product. | High

C-6 | A7.01 Sheet 1, Kitchen North Elevation; Specifications page 6 | Kitchen elevation A7.01/1 dimensions the backsplash as "SS-1 6MM," specifying a 6mm thickness, while the project specifications list backsplash SS-1 thickness as "TBD" — the drawings establish a thickness the specifications explicitly leave unresolved. | High

C-8 | A8.01 Door Schedule, Door 15-1; Specifications page 2 | The project specifications describe the garage door as a "Clopay Modern Steel Door - Grooved with Long Panel Windows," indicating a door with integral glazing panels, but the door schedule on A8.01 lists the garage door material simply as "METAL" with no notation of glazing — if the Clopay door has windows, this affects the door's thermal and visual performance characteristics. | Moderate

### D — Building Envelope and Building Science

D-1 | A4.00 E-1 Wall Assembly | The E-1 CMU foundation wall assembly shows only a parge coat as the exterior below-grade treatment, with no waterproofing membrane, drainage board, or drain mat on the exterior face of the CMU below grade; a parge coat alone is not a waterproofing system and is insufficient for below-grade moisture control in a high-rainfall climate zone (4A, central NC). | High

D-2 | A4.00 E-1 Wall Assembly; A4.01 R-1 Roof Assembly | The CMU crawl space wall assembly shows 1/2" rigid insulation on the interior face (approximately R2.5), while the code requires R-10 at crawl space walls; this is separately confirmed by the project specifications requiring 2" XPS — the assembly as drawn will not meet code thermal requirements for the enclosed crawl space. | Certain

D-3 | A4.01 R-1 Roof Assembly | The R-1 roof assembly places the weather barrier layer below the plywood sheathing (between the plywood and the TJI joists) rather than above the sheathing between the sheathing and the rigid insulation; placing the weather/air barrier below the structural sheathing means any moisture entering through the standing seam roof, the air gap, or the rigid insulation layer will be trapped at the plywood with no drainage path. | High

D-4 | A4.11 Sheet 5, Base Exterior | The base exterior detail (A4.11/5) shows the transition from exterior cladding to the foundation, with parge coat and insect screen but no visible capillary break or through-wall flashing at the wood sill plate level to prevent moisture wicking from the CMU to the wood framing above. | Moderate

## Summary

This is a conceptually strong and architecturally ambitious residential project, but the construction documents exhibit significant coordination failures that would generate a high volume of RFIs and change orders before and during construction. The drawing set and specifications were not reconciled prior to issue: the window schedule specifies Marvin Ultrex while the specifications require Andersen 400 Series — a fundamental product conflict affecting every window in the project. The exterior cladding specification simultaneously assigns two different products to the west facade. The crawl space insulation as drawn achieves approximately R2.5 against a code requirement of R10 and a specification requirement of R10 using 2" XPS. The stair geometry as labeled on A3.22 is geometrically impossible, with implied riser heights of 15" on the south section, and the riser count and rise dimensions on the east section produce a total rise that does not match the labeled floor-to-floor dimension. Several referenced sheets are missing entirely (A8.10) or exist under a different number than the index states (A5.11 vs. A5.20). The three most critical areas of concern are: **(1) the specification-drawing product conflicts** (windows, cladding, insulation) that cannot be resolved without owner direction; **(2) the stair geometry**, which must be re-detailed before structural framing begins; and **(3) the crawl space wall insulation**, where the drawn assembly fails to meet code by a factor of four and must be corrected before the foundation work is permitted.
