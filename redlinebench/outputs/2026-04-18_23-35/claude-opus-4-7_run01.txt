# Architectural Document Review — Bonfire House

## Step 1: Project Context

- **Project:** Bonfire House, unaddressed parcel, Chatham County, NC (Residential District 1)
- **Codes:** 2018 NC Residential Code (adopts IRC 2015 w/ amendments); Building Inspector jurisdiction Chatham County
- **Building Type:** Single-family R-3, Type V-B construction; 4 BR / 3.5 BA; 3,270 SF heated + 570 SF garage; 2 stories, 27'-11" height
- **Climate Zone:** 4A (per G0.11)
- **Drawing Set Scope:** General (G0.01, G0.11, G1.00) and Architectural (A1.00 – A8.01) only. Structural, civil, MEP, landscape, and pool drawings are referenced but NOT included. Survey page is intentionally blank.
- **Specifications:** 11-page narrative specification provided (not formatted as CSI MasterFormat divisions)

## Summary

The drawing set demonstrates reasonable graphic quality and a clear design intent, but it contains significant coordination gaps that would generate numerous RFIs and change orders during construction. The most serious issues are (1) **specification-to-drawing misalignment** — window manufacturer conflict (Andersen vs. Marvin), siding location conflict (West assigned to both lap siding and wood cladding), and multiple discrepancies in insulation thickness/R-value between spec, code summary, and drawn assemblies; (2) **building-science concerns in the exterior envelope** — a double vapor barrier sandwich at the CMU crawl wall, missing WRB on the fiberboard wall type, a hybrid roof assembly whose exterior/cavity insulation ratio and WRB placement are unconventional for Climate Zone 4A, and a 1/4"/ft low-slope roof over the entry hall with a standing-seam product whose minimum slope must be verified; and (3) **code and life-safety documentation gaps** — no fire-rated assembly is identified for garage/dwelling separation, EERO compliance is not verifiable from the window schedule (most units are fixed), stair geometry (riser count × riser height does not reach the 10'-0" floor-to-floor), headroom, handrails, and make-up air for the 650 CFM range hood are all unaddressed. Secondary concerns include extensive "TBD" deferrals, the deferred pool/retaining-wall design, and an incomplete door hardware schedule. The set would benefit from a coordination pass before bidding, with particular attention to reconciling the specification and drawings on windows, insulation, and cladding locations, and to resolving code-driven items that currently appear deferred.
